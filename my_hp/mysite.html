A Chemical Engineering PhD. Candidate (minor in Computer Science), who is also enrolled in a diploma program in Material Science and Engineering at Louisiana State University. My current research focusses on engineering the electronic structure of high entropy alloys (CrFeCoNi) for electro-catalytic applications, as a possible replacement for the more expensive precious metals (Pt, Pd, Ru, Rh, Os, Au, Ag).
Skills acquired:
  • Alloy design (superalloys, microalloys, highentropy alloys)
  • Computational catalyst design
  • Ab-initio computations using VASP
  • Atomic simulation environment (ASE)
  • Atomistic machine learning package (AMP)
  • Working with linux and high performance computing (HPC)
  • Python programming language
  • Monte Carlo methods
As a Material Scientist, my focus is on the electronic and geometric structure of materials and how they affect their bulk and surface properties. Good knowledge of microfabrication processes such as photolithography, spin coating, masking, and etching, inline with the megaposit SPR 3000 series photoresist specifications.
Courses taken:
  • Condensed matter physics
  • Statistical thermodynamics
  • Computational physics
  • Advanced spectroscopy
  • Integrated circuit engineering
My focus as a computer scientist is on developing and deploying optimized models for end users, utilizing cloud services (Iaas and Paas).
Skills acquired:
